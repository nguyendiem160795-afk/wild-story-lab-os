# DOC-008 – Story Framework Standard

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Approved  
**Owner:** Wild Story Lab  
**Module:** Story OS

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official storytelling framework for all Wild Story Lab productions.

Every story, regardless of theme, should follow this structure to maintain engagement, emotional consistency, and audience retention.

---

# Core Story Principles

Every story should be:

- Easy to understand for children aged 3–8.
- Positive and encouraging.
- Emotionally engaging.
- Visually dynamic.
- Suitable for short-form video.

---

# Story Formula

Every story follows this sequence:

1. Hook
2. Character Introduction
3. Goal
4. Challenge
5. Escalation
6. Climax
7. Resolution
8. Happy Ending

---

# Scene Breakdown

## Scene 1 – Hook

Duration: 3–5 seconds

Objective:

Capture attention immediately with movement, surprise, or curiosity.

---

## Scene 2 – Introduce Mochi

Show:

- Personality
- Emotion
- Current situation

Audience should instantly connect with Mochi.

---

## Scene 3 – Goal

Clearly establish what Mochi wants to achieve.

Examples:

- Learn a new word
- Cook a meal
- Help a friend
- Find an object

---

## Scene 4 – Challenge

Introduce a simple obstacle.

The challenge should be understandable for young children.

---

## Scene 5 – Escalation

Increase excitement through action, humor, or unexpected events.

---

## Scene 6 – Climax

The most emotional or exciting moment.

Mochi faces the biggest challenge.

---

## Scene 7 – Resolution

Problem is solved through kindness, creativity, or teamwork.

---

## Scene 8 – Happy Ending

End with:

- Smile
- Celebration
- Lesson learned
- Positive emotion

---

# Educational Layer

Every story should include at least one learning objective.

Examples:

- English vocabulary
- Numbers
- Colors
- Shapes
- Animals
- Good habits
- Friendship
- Creativity

---

# Emotional Curve

Recommended emotional flow:

Curiosity

↓

Excitement

↓

Tension

↓

Hope

↓

Joy

---

# Story Rules

Always:

- Keep Mochi as the emotional center.
- One main objective.
- One clear conflict.
- One satisfying resolution.

Never:

- Use horror.
- Use violence.
- Include inappropriate language.
- Overcomplicate the plot.

---

# Story Checklist

Before production, confirm:

- Strong Hook.
- Clear Goal.
- Understandable Challenge.
- Emotional Climax.
- Positive Ending.
- Educational Value.
- Suitable for children.
