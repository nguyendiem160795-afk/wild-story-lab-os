# Purpose

Knowledge Governance defines the policies, responsibilities, approval processes, and lifecycle management practices that ensure the quality, integrity, security, and sustainability of every Knowledge Object within Wild Story Lab OS.

Governance ensures that knowledge remains accurate, trusted, version-controlled, and reusable across all production activities.

---

# Objectives

- Maintain knowledge quality
- Define ownership
- Standardize approval workflows
- Protect knowledge integrity
- Enable continuous improvement
- Support AI-safe knowledge management

---

# Governance Principles

Knowledge is an organizational asset.

Every Knowledge Object must have an owner.

Every change must be traceable.

Every approval must be accountable.

Every published object must meet quality standards.

# Governance Roles

## Knowledge Owner

Responsible for

- Content accuracy
- Lifecycle management
- Version approval
- Maintenance planning

---

## Reviewer

Responsible for

- Technical validation
- Editorial review
- Educational review
- Consistency verification

---

## Approver

Responsible for

- Final approval
- Release authorization
- Governance compliance

---

## AI Agent

Responsible for

- Metadata generation
- Dependency analysis
- Duplicate detection
- Recommendation generation

AI Agents cannot approve production knowledge.

---

## Administrator

Responsible for

- Governance policies
- Access control
- Audit management
- System configuration

# Ownership Rules

Every Knowledge Object must define

- Primary Owner
- Backup Owner
- Review Frequency
- Approval Authority
- Stewardship Status

---

# Review Schedule

Critical Objects

Every 3 months

---

Production Standards

Every 6 months

---

Educational Content

Every 6 months

---

Reference Library

Every 12 months

---

Archived Objects

No scheduled review

---

# Compliance Requirements

Every published object must

✓ Have an owner

✓ Have a version

✓ Pass QA

✓ Be searchable

✓ Be linked to the Knowledge Graph

✓ Record change history

# Risk Management

Monitor

- Orphan Knowledge Objects
- Missing Owners
- Expired Reviews
- Broken Dependencies
- Unapproved References
- Version Conflicts

---

# Best Practices

- Assign ownership immediately.
- Review knowledge regularly.
- Archive obsolete objects.
- Maintain complete audit trails.
- Automate repetitive governance tasks where appropriate.
