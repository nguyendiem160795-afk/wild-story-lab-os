# Camera Reference

## Extreme Close-Up

Purpose

Highlight tiny details or strong emotions.

Best For

- Eyes
- Smile
- Tears
- Small objects
- Surprise

Avoid

Large environments.

Example Prompt

Extreme close-up of Mochi's sparkling green eyes as he discovers a glowing magic book.

---

## Close-Up

Purpose

Focus on facial expression.

Best For

- Dialogue
- Emotional moments
- Character introduction

Avoid

Large action scenes.

Example Prompt

Close-up of Mochi smiling warmly while greeting the audience.

---

## Medium Shot

Purpose

Show gestures and body language.

Best For

- Teaching
- Explaining
- Daily activities

Example Prompt

Medium shot of Mochi standing beside the classroom whiteboard and waving happily.

---

## Wide Shot

Purpose

Reveal environment.

Best For

- Classroom
- Forest
- Kitchen
- Playground

Example Prompt

Wide shot of Mochi walking through a colorful magical classroom filled with toys.

---

## Tracking Shot

Purpose

Follow moving characters.

Best For

- Running
- Walking
- Adventure

Example Prompt

Tracking shot following Mochi as he runs through the enchanted forest.

---

## Orbit Shot

Purpose

Create cinematic energy.

Best For

- Character reveal
- Hero moments
- Magical scenes

Example Prompt

Smooth orbit shot around Mochi as glowing stars appear around him.

---

## Crane Shot

Purpose

Reveal scale and transition between scenes.

Best For

- Ending scenes
- Scene transitions
- World building

Example Prompt

Crane shot rising above Mochi to reveal the entire magical school.

---

# Camera Selection Guide

| Scene Type | Recommended Cameras |
|------------|---------------------|
| Introduction | Wide Shot + Dolly In |
| Teaching | Medium Shot + Close-Up |
| Dialogue | Close-Up |
| Emotional | Extreme Close-Up |
| Comedy | Medium Shot |
| Action | Tracking Shot |
| Adventure | Orbit + Crane |
| Ending | Wide Shot + Pull Out |

---

# Camera Movement Rules

✓ Every movement must support storytelling.

✓ Keep movements smooth.

✓ Avoid excessive camera motion.

✓ Prioritize visual clarity for children.

✓ Use one primary movement per shot whenever possible.

---

# Common Mistakes

Do not:

- Randomly switch camera angles.
- Combine too many movements in one shot.
- Use handheld camera for calm educational scenes.
- Overuse orbit shots.
- Zoom without narrative purpose.

---

# Wild Story Lab Camera Style

Preferred Style

- Bright
- Smooth
- Cinematic
- Child-friendly
- Pixar-inspired
- Disney-inspired

Avoid

- Horror camera language
- Violent handheld motion
- Shaky footage
- Excessive Dutch angles
