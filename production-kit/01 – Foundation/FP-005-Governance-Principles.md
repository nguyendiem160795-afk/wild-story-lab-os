# Governance Principles (Nguyên lý Quản trị)

> Canonical governance principles for the Enterprise AI Operating System Specification (EAOSS).

---

# 1. Purpose (Mục đích)

This document defines the governance principles that ensure EAOSS remains consistent, reliable, and maintainable throughout its lifecycle.

Governance establishes how decisions are made, how changes are approved, and how repository integrity is preserved.

---

# 2. Objectives (Mục tiêu)

These principles aim to:

- Ensure architectural integrity.
- Define decision authority.
- Standardize change management.
- Preserve repository quality.
- Maintain traceability.
- Support sustainable evolution.

---

# 3. Scope (Phạm vi)

These principles apply to:

- Repository governance
- Module governance
- Documentation governance
- Standards management
- Policy management
- Architecture governance
- Future EAOSS extensions

---

# 4. Principle 1 — Clear Ownership

Every artifact shall have an identified owner.

Ownership includes responsibility for:

- Accuracy
- Maintenance
- Review coordination
- Version management
- Deprecation decisions

Ownership shall always be explicit.

---

# 5. Principle 2 — Controlled Change

No significant change shall occur without formal review.

Changes affecting architecture, standards, or governance require documented approval before becoming part of the official baseline.

---

# 6. Principle 3 — Accountability

All major decisions shall identify:

- Decision owner
- Decision date
- Decision rationale
- Expected impact

Decision accountability shall remain permanently traceable.

---

# 7. Principle 4 — Transparency

Governance processes shall be transparent.

Repository users shall be able to understand:

- Why decisions were made
- What changed
- When changes occurred
- Who approved them

---

# 8. Principle 5 — Baseline Integrity

Published baselines are stable references.

Changes to a baseline require:

- Review
- Approval
- Version update
- Changelog entry

Approved baselines shall never be modified silently.

---

# 9. Principle 6 — Consistent Decision Process

Equivalent governance decisions shall follow the same review and approval workflow.

Consistency improves fairness, predictability, and auditability.

---

# 10. Principle 7 — Policy Compliance

Every module shall comply with approved repository policies.

Exceptions shall:

- Be documented
- Be justified
- Be approved
- Include an expiration or review condition where applicable

---

# 11. Principle 8 — Continuous Governance

Governance evolves alongside the repository.

Policies and standards should be reviewed periodically to ensure continued relevance.

---

# 12. Principle 9 — Risk Awareness

Governance decisions shall consider:

- Architectural risks
- Operational risks
- Documentation risks
- Long-term maintenance risks

Risk assessment should accompany significant changes.

---

# 13. Principle 10 — Auditability

Governance activities shall produce records that enable independent review.

Artifacts supporting auditability include:

- Changelogs
- Review reports
- Baselines
- Approval records
- Version histories

---

# 14. Governance Checklist

Every governance decision should satisfy the following checklist:

| Requirement | Mandatory |
|-------------|-----------|
| Owner identified | Yes |
| Scope defined | Yes |
| Decision rationale documented | Yes |
| Impact assessed | Yes |
| Review completed | Yes |
| Approval recorded | Yes |
| Changelog updated | Yes |

---

# 15. Compliance

All governance activities within EAOSS shall comply with these principles.

Compliance shall be verified during Governance Review and Compliance Review.
