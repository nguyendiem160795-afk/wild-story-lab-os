# 1. Purpose

This document defines the governance framework for all Operational Playbooks within Wild Story Lab OS.

The Governance Framework establishes policies, roles, responsibilities, approval processes, and operational controls that ensure Playbooks remain accurate, trustworthy, maintainable, and compliant throughout their lifecycle.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Governance Framework aims to:

- Protect the integrity of Playbooks.
- Standardize decision-making.
- Ensure accountability.
- Preserve operational quality.
- Support AI-assisted governance.
- Maintain long-term consistency.
- Enable controlled evolution.

---

# 3. Governance Principles

Governance shall be based on the following principles.

- Transparency
- Accountability
- Traceability
- Consistency
- Controlled Change
- Separation of Responsibilities
- Continuous Improvement

Governance applies equally to human and AI-assisted operations.

---

# 4. Governance Scope

Governance applies to:

- Playbook creation
- Review
- Validation
- Approval
- Release
- Version updates
- Retirement
- Archive management

No lifecycle stage is exempt from governance.

---

# 5. Governance Roles

The following governance roles are defined.

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Author | Create and maintain Playbooks |
| Reviewer | Verify quality and compliance |
| Approver | Grant official approval |
| Maintainer | Manage ongoing updates |
| Librarian | Maintain indexing and metadata |
| Automation Owner | Govern automation behavior |
| AI Agent | Execute approved Playbooks within defined limits |

Each role shall have clearly defined authority.

---

# 6. Decision Authority

Governance decisions include:

- Approve new Playbooks.
- Reject non-compliant Playbooks.
- Approve major revisions.
- Retire obsolete Playbooks.
- Resolve governance conflicts.
- Approve taxonomy changes.

Authority shall be explicitly assigned.

---

# 7. Approval Workflow

The standard approval workflow is:

```text
Draft

↓

Author Review

↓

Peer Review

↓

Validation

↓

Governance Approval

↓

Release
```

No Playbook shall be released without governance approval.

---

# 8. Change Governance

All changes shall be classified.

| Change Type | Approval Required |
|-------------|------------------|
| Patch | Maintainer |
| Minor | Reviewer + Maintainer |
| Major | Governance Approval |
| Breaking Change | Governance Board |

Change records shall remain permanently traceable.

---

# 9. Policy Compliance

Every Playbook shall comply with:

- Playbook Standard
- Architecture Standard
- Validation System
- Version Control
- Quality System
- Automation System

Non-compliant Playbooks shall not be released.

---

# 10. Governance Records

Governance activities shall record:

- Decision ID
- Decision date
- Responsible role
- Affected Playbook
- Decision summary
- Approval outcome

Governance records support auditing and historical analysis.

---

# 11. Exception Management

Governance exceptions shall document:

- Reason
- Scope
- Risk assessment
- Temporary controls
- Expiration date
- Approval authority

Permanent undocumented exceptions are prohibited.

---

# 12. AI Governance

AI systems shall:

- Follow approved Playbooks.
- Respect approval gates.
- Preserve execution records.
- Explain recommendations.
- Never override governance decisions.

AI operates within governance, not above it.

---

# 13. Governance Metrics

Recommended governance metrics include:

| Metric | Target |
|---------|---------|
| Governance Compliance | 100% |
| Approval Completion | 100% |
| Unauthorized Changes | 0 |
| Audit Success Rate | 100% |
| Exception Resolution | ≥95% |
| Policy Compliance | 100% |

Metrics support governance effectiveness.

---

# 14. Continuous Governance

Governance should evolve through:

- Periodic policy reviews.
- Architecture updates.
- Audit findings.
- AI recommendations.
- Operational feedback.
- Regulatory or organizational changes.

Improvements shall preserve backward compatibility where practical.

# 16. Success Criteria

The Governance Framework is successful when:

- Every Playbook follows approved governance.
- Responsibilities are clearly defined.
- Changes are fully traceable.
- AI respects governance boundaries.
- Operational integrity is maintained.
- Governance scales with library growth.
